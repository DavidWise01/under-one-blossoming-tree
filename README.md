UNDER ONE BLOSSOMING TREE — David Lee Wise on the Heian women who built written Japanese. Barred from prestige Chinese, court women were left with the new KANA syllabary (the "woman's hand") and with it founded a literature six centuries before Europe: ⚑ Murasaki Shikibu (源氏物語 The Tale of Genji, c.1008 — the first PSYCHOLOGICAL novel, narrated interiority), Sei Shōnagon (枕草子 The Pillow Book — the zuihitsu observation-essay), Izumi Shikibu (the intimate poetic diary + 600 waka; three of her best rendered plain beneath her 11th-c. originals). A GENERATION not a bloodline (rival empresses' courts); transmission = kana/Kokinshū 905 → these three → inheritors (Murasaki's daughter; the Sarashina diarist). ⚑ HONEST scope-flag: "you can read gender from the text" is true ONLY in-band, because Heian assigned scripts by sex (kana=women); out of band the claim fails. Founding dyad surfaced in the thread: 心 kokoro (heart) / 言葉 kotoba (word) from Ki no Tsurayuki's 905 Kana Preface — heart=seed, words=leaves. Washi/murasaki/sakura design, falling-petal canvas.
